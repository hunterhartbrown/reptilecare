Eastern Collared Lizard Care Guide

Quick, trustworthy care info for Eastern Collared Lizards.

Quick Facts

Eastern collared lizards are active, vividly colored diurnal lizards from the semi‑arid Southwest and northern Mexico. Their bold patterns and quick, bipedal sprints make them captivating, but they do best with experienced keepers due to their energy and space needs.

House a single adult in at least a 40‑gallon breeder (36″ × 18″ × 18″) with deep, sandy soil for digging and sturdy rocks and branches for climbing (ReptiFiles). Pricing varies by locality and availability.

Species overview

Eastern collared lizards (Crotaphytus collaris) inhabit rocky outcrops, desert scrub, and grasslands where they bask in intense sun and sprint to evade predators. Adults reach 8–14″ and are highly territorial; house singly.

Habitat and size

Use a front‑opening terrarium of at least 36″ × 18″ × 18″; larger (48″ × 24″ × 24″) is ideal for movement. Provide secure ventilation and escape‑proof lids.

Line with ~4″ of a 60/40 topsoil–play‑sand mix for digging. Include a warm‑side and cool‑side hide and a flat basking rock beneath the heat lamp.

Heating

Create a gradient with a basking spot of 105–115°F and a cool zone of 75–85°F. Night lows of 68–75°F are acceptable (ReptiFiles).

Provide strong full‑spectrum lighting with a T5‑HO UVB to reach a UV index near 4–6 at the basking site, plus a 6500 K daylight on a 12‑hour cycle.

Humidity and airflow

Maintain ambient humidity at 30–50% with strong ventilation to prevent respiratory issues.

Provide a humid hide (~70% RH) on the cool side using damp sphagnum moss; allow drying between light misting.

Food

Feed a variety of gut‑loaded insects such as crickets, dubia roaches, and locusts—daily for juveniles; adults 3–4×/week. Dust with calcium with D₃ and provide a weekly multivitamin.

Offer fresh dechlorinated water daily; light morning misting can encourage drinking from surfaces.

Enrichment

Include secure rock piles and sturdy branches for climbing and perching. Provide hides on both warm and cool sides (ReptiFiles).

Add leaf litter or artificial plants for digging and cover. Scatter live prey for hunting, and occasionally rearrange decor for novelty.
