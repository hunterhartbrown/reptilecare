Eastern Collared Lizard Care Guide

Eastern Collared Lizards (Crotaphytus spp.) are medium-sized lizards native to the American Southwest, Mexico, and the Baja peninsula. Recognizable by their vibrant hues and distinct dark “collars,” they are highly active and thrive in hot, rocky habitats. With proper care, they can live for over 10 years.

Collared lizards display a spectrum of colors—from tan or gray to bright aquamarine and yellow—often with speckling and dark neck-stripe patterns. Males typically exhibit more vivid coloration than females.

Provide at least a 36″ L × 18″ W × 16″ H (90 × 45 × 45 cm) enclosure with front-opening doors for easy access. Active climbers benefit from extra floor space when possible.

Mimic rocky terrain with sturdy branches, rock ledges, and platforms. Use a natural substrate—clean topsoil mixed with fine sand—packed about 4″ deep for burrowing.

Maintain a basking spot of 104–118 °F (40–48 °C) and a cooler zone of 77–90 °F (25–32 °C). Nighttime temps may drop to 68–79 °F (20–26 °C). A halogen heat lamp (2 × 50 W PAR30 bulbs) about 8–12″ above the basking area works well.

Keep ambient humidity low—30–50%—with good airflow to prevent mold. A digital hygrometer helps monitor levels. Offer a slightly humid hide on the cool side during shedding.

Insectivorous by nature—feed gut-loaded crickets, mealworms, dubia roaches daily, dusted with calcium and vitamin powders. Occasional small vertebrates or plant matter may be offered.

Reach lengths of 8–14″ (20–36 cm). With optimal care, Eastern Collared Lizards often live beyond 10 years.

Males display brighter colors and hemipenal bulges at the tail base. They are territorial—keep males separate or with multiple females in a spacious enclosure to minimize conflict.
