Eastern Collared Lizard Care Guide

Eastern Collared Lizards (Crotaphytus spp.) are medium-sized lizards native to the American Southwest, Mexico, and the Baja peninsula. Recognizable by their vibrant hues and distinct dark “collars,” they are highly active and can live for over 10 years with proper care.

Provide at least a 36″ L × 18″ W × 16″ H (90 × 45 × 45 cm) enclosure with front-opening doors for easy access. These active climbers benefit from extra floor space whenever possible.

Maintain a basking spot of 104–118 °F (40–48 °C) and a cooler zone of 77–90 °F (25–32 °C). Night temperatures can drop to 68–79 °F (20–26 °C). A halogen heat lamp (2 × 50 W PAR30 bulbs) positioned 8–12″ above the basking area works well.

Keep ambient humidity low (30–50%) with good airflow to prevent mold. Offer a slightly humid hide on the cool side during shedding; monitor with a digital hygrometer.

Primarily insectivorous—feed gut-loaded crickets, mealworms, and dubia roaches daily, dusted with calcium and vitamin supplements.

Include sturdy branches and rock ledges to mimic their natural habitat and promote climbing and hiding behavior.
