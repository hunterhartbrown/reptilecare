Eastern Collared Lizard Care Guide

Eastern Collared Lizards (Crotaphytus collaris) are diurnal, medium-sized lizards found in the south-central United States and northern Mexico, known for their vivid colors and dark “collar” markings. Adults reach 8–14″ (20–35 cm) in length and have powerful jaws for subduing prey (ReptiFiles).

In nature they inhabit rocky outcrops, desert scrub, and open grasslands, often basking on sun-lit stones. Their impressive bipedal sprinting (up to ~16 mph) helps them evade predators. Highly territorial and solitary, males may fight or cannibalize tankmates; best housed alone. Lifespan in captivity exceeds 10 years with proper care.

Provide at least a 36″ L × 18″ W × 18″ H front-opening glass or PVC terrarium. Larger enclosures (48″×24″×24″) enable more exercise and reduce stress.

Use 4″ (10 cm) of substrate—a 60/40 topsoil–play-sand mix—to allow natural digging. Spot-clean daily, replace fully every 3–4 months, and switch to paper towels if health issues arise. Include two hides (warm/cool) and a flat basking rock.

Create a gradient: basking spot of 105–115 °F (40–46 °C) using a 50–75 W halogen flood lamp 8–12″ above a flat rock, and a cool zone of 75–85 °F (24–29 °C). Night lows of 68–75 °F (20–24 °C) are acceptable (ReptiFiles).

Install a T5 HO 10.0 UVB tube above the basking area (6–8″ distance) to deliver a UV index of ~4–6. Supplement with a 6500 K daylight lamp on a 12 h/12 h timer (Arcadia).

Aim for ambient humidity of 30–50% and ensure strong airflow via a mesh top or side vents to prevent respiratory issues (ReptiFiles).

Offer a humid hide (~70% RH) using damp sphagnum moss on the cool side; mist lightly and allow drying within hours to avoid mold.

Feed a varied array of gut-loaded insects—crickets, Dubia roaches, locusts, superworms—daily for juveniles; adults 3–4×/week. Release prey for hunting enrichment.

Dust feeders with calcium (+D₃) at every feeding and a reptile multivitamin once weekly. Offer pinkie mice or small anoles as treats monthly for adults.

Provide fresh dechlorinated water in a shallow dish daily and mist decor in the morning to encourage droplet drinking.

Include secure rock piles and sturdy branches for climbing and perching. Provide hides on both warm and cool sides (ReptiFiles).

Add leaf litter or artificial plants for digging and cover. Scatter live prey for hunting, and occasionally rearrange decor for novelty.
